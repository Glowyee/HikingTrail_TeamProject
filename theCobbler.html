General information

The Cobbler makes for a fantastic short day out. It is very popular due to its distinctive outline and its closeness to the city of Glasgow. The path to the peak has been improved in recent years. The first part of the route is an initial zig zag and an ascent. This is followed by a relatively flat walk all the way to the bottom of the Cobbler where you are faced with an ascent. Depending on the day the weather might get colder and colder as you approach it and the Cobbler might even still be covered in snow. It is not unusual for the Cobbler to be snowy in May!

Terrain

Clear paths with some steep rocky sections particularly on the descent.

Getting there

The train and local buses will take you close to the Succoth car park, Arrochar which is the starting point.
