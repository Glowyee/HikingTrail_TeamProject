General information

Ben Lomond is one of the most popular hikes in the UK. It’s a relatively challenging route with a spectacular view of other Munros on one side and of Loch Lomond on the other side. There are two routes which can be taken in order to hike it. They are both split into steep ascents followed by a plain area and ending with another steep ascent to the peak. Generally, people ascend to the peak on one of the routes and descend on the other route. Both routes take you to the starting point.

Terrain

While most of the path is well defined, some parts are narrow and rocky.

Getting there

Unfortunately the way to get there is by car. Alternatively, you could walk or cycle from Balmaha. Keep in mind that walking to the starting point from Balmaha takes approximately 2 hours 30 minutes. The starting point is the Ben Lomond car park.
