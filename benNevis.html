Ben Nevis

  • Elevation: 1352m
  • Distance: 17km
  • Time: 6-8 hours
  • Difficulty: 3
  • Start grid reference: NN122730

General information

Ben Nevis is the highest mountain in the UK. The path to the peak can be challenging at times. The first part of the route is an initial zig zag and an ascent, followed by a relatively flat walk. The last part of the hike, part which makes up almost half of the route is an ascent towards the peak which slowly increases in difficulty. Depending on the time of the year, you can easily find snow on the peak. For example, snow storms were observed on its peak in April despite there being no snow on the route to the peak or in the surrounding areas.

Terrain

Clear paths with a steep rocky section particularly close to the peak. The path also becomes narrower on the third section of the hike.

Getting there

If not driving, the easiest way to get to Ben Nevis is from Fort William which can be reached from other areas around Scotland by either coach or train. Bus N42 runs from Fort William to the starting point - Ben Nevis Visitor centre - from May to October. Alternatively, a 20 minute taxi ride from Fort William will take you to the starting point of the hike. If travelling by car, drive to the start point which has a car park that you can use.
